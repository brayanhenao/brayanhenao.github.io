Travel experience



The trip starts in Neiva, on December 27, 2015 at 5:00 A.M., we start in 3 cars (a total of 12 people) to Santa Marta. For the duration of the trip in each car were 2 people able to drive, in my case I was traveling with my dad who relieved me in certain sections so that the fatigue was not so much. As it was a family trip, the idea was to stop at places we did not know, so the trip that normally lasts 18-19 hours (one way) was planned for 1 day and a half counting different stops to eat or meet places.


The first stop was in Honda, municipality of Tolima, to have lunch and visit the city. It is a small but cozy city, leaving aside its hot weather (around 34 ° C) has old structures, but its main tourist attraction is the Navarro Bridge, an old structure that has the title of the first bridge built in Latin America with a structure of iron, which we visited.


The second stop was in Aguachica, municipality of Cesar, where we had planned to dine and get a place to sleep. Since the day before I was indisposed for a meal that had fallen ill, so the first thing I did when I arrived was to look for a hospital in which they treated me, as it was a town the indications of the people were places instead of directions like "next to" "behind x part" and we got lost, finally I got a drugstore and bought a serum and pasta. Talking about the place to sleep, we got a very basic but incredibly cheap hotel, called Brisas del Cesar, because the only objective was to sleep and get up early to continue the trip the next day, so it did not make sense to pay for an expensive and luxurious hotel.


The third stop was in Curumaní, municipality of Cesar, where we had planned to have breakfast. We stopped at a place which was quite full (which for my dad means good food) and ordered breakfast for everyone. At the end we were surprised at how cheap it went, since it was a popular restaurant, and that the food was very good. The breakfast for all (12) came out roughly 40,000 pesos.


The last stop before reaching Santa Marta was in Aracataca, municipality of Magdalena, where we stopped to eat something and get to know the city. Like many cities on the Caribbean coast, people are very friendly and helpful. We visited the Gabriel Garcia Márquez house museum to learn a little about the origins of this great Colombian writer.


This ended this trip to the Caribbean coast, although it was long and exhausting, it was fun to discover new places that I did not know.
